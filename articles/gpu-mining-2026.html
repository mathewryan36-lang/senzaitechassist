Is GPU Mining Still Worth It in 2026?

Published: December 2025 | Author: Lucius Prime

GPU mining has gone through major shifts post-Ethereum. But in 2026, it’s far from dead. While competition is fierce and electricity rates play a critical role, there are still real profits to be made — if you know where to look.

What Coins Can You Still Mine?

  • Kaspa (KAS): One of the fastest PoW blockchains — still profitable for solo miners with GPUs.
  • Ergo (ERG): Smart contract-capable and eco-conscious. Low difficulty, decent returns.
  • Neoxa (NEOX): A GPU-friendly blockchain built for gaming/metaverse integration.
  • Radiant (RXD): Growing fast. Optimized for GPU miners with a strong community.

Recommended Hardware for 2026

While older cards like the RTX 3060 Ti still perform well on select coins, newer models offer better efficiency. Here are our top picks:

  • RTX 4070 Ti SUPER: Best value/performance balance for small-to-mid farms.
  • RX 7900 XTX: AMD’s flagship still holding strong for Kaspa and ERG.
  • Used RTX 3080s: Secondary market deals are ideal for budget miners.

Mining Profitability in 2026

Profit margins are tighter — but with cheap electricity or solar setups, it's still viable. For solo miners, it’s more about long-term stacking than short-term flipping.

Use profitability calculators like WhatToMine to adjust expectations based on current hash rates and difficulty.

Future-Proof Your Mining Rig

  • Use open-air rigs or server chassis for better airflow and thermal control.
  • Keep your BIOS + drivers updated for efficiency boosts.
  • Explore merged mining and multi-coin switching software like HiveOS or RaveOS.

Conclusion

GPU mining in 2026 isn’t dead — but it’s evolved. Smart miners focus on efficiency, long-term holdings, and hybrid setups. At Senzai Tech Assist, we’re still running test rigs and stacking where it makes sense.
